Chess 101 for Dummies
Welcome to Chess Bootcamp. This Website aims to teach new players who have recently discovered chess through media, all they need to know to get started playing. With the recent resurgence of the game due to popularity of the Netflix’s show “The Queens Gambit” and chess grandmasters such as “Hikaru Nakamura” and “Daniel Naroditsky” streaming on Twitch and YouTube, there have been many new players to the world of chess, and the goal of this website is to accompany them in the start of their new hobby. This website will cover topics from history to the basics, to the strategy behind the game, and the world of chess.
What is Chess?

Chess is an abstract strategy game and involves no hidden information. It is played on a checker board with 64 squares arranged in an 8x8 grid. Each player has a King, a Queen, 2 Bishops, 2 Horses, 2 Rooks, and 8 Pawns, and the objective of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king.


Competitions are governed internationally by FIDE (International Chess Federation). Since the inception of the game, Theory has been developed. The game is considered an art form and has connections with many other fields such as math, computer science, and psychology.
