FIDE

The internation governing body for chess is known by its French acronym FIDE (Federation Internationale Des Eschecs). Over 180 countries' chess organizations joined FIDE, and the group also includes non-national organizations such as the blind, the deaf and other disabilities. It is acknowledged as a sports governing body by the International Olympic Committee. FIDE is responsible for organizing the World Chess Championship, the current World Champion being Magnus Carlsen of Norway, and Women's World Champion Ju Wenjun of China.




Tournaments and Matches

It is a very organized sport, structured international and national leagues, tournaments and congress. Thousands of tournaments are held every year around the world catering to players of all levels. There are 2 main tournament formats. Round-Robin where every player plays a game against every other player, and the Swiss system which matches players of the same score across different rounds.




Titles and Rankings

Chess Organizations use the Elo rating system developed by Arpad Elo. Players who reach certain milestones are awarded lifetime titles by FIDE. Grandmaster the highest title a player can attain apart from World Champion, requires a minimum of 2500 elo rating and "norms" which are gotten when you travel internationally and are able to beat players who are already grandmasters. Internation Master's conditions are similar to GM apart from the minimum elo rating being 2400, FIDE Master is achieved by having a rating of 2300+ and candidate master who needs a rating of atleast 2200. There are also separate women's titles which require 200 elo rating points lower compared to their counterpart.
